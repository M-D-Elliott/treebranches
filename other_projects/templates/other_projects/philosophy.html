What is the meaning of life?

Futurism

Futurism is the philosophy of perceiving the impact of technological advancements on civil or social hierarchies.

Macroscopic Ethics

Success of an organism relative to other organisms can be most easily measured by the scope of the environment each occupies. In other words, the larger the area of influence of an organism, intelligent or otherwise, the more relative survival success its species may claim.

When considering a civilization that succeeds even on the global scale, e.g. humans, the impact of that civilization on all "lower" forms of life is considerable. So, just as humans impact countless microscopic organisms on Earth, civilizations of Type I and higher would undoubtedly impact countless potential life below them. From an ethical perspective, this impact bears import.

The Kardashev Scale

The Kardashev scale is a hypothetical model by which a civilization's degree of energy access can be used to reverse-engineer the technological capabilities and social necessities of that civilization. Hypothetical civilizations are divided into types.

Kardashev Civilization Types I through III can evolve through simple laws of physics over a long period of time. The Type III Civilization may evolve over tens of thousands of years or millions of years depending on whether or not time travel is possible. The Kardashev Civilizations of Type IV and V can only evolve if time travel is possible.

Methods: A Type I Civilization starts its life upon gaining access to energy equivalent to all energy that arrives on its home planet from its home star, or e^17 Watts. This can also be achieved by anachronistic technology such as free vaccuum energy.

Ethics: Even at its inception such a civilization is already capable of determining the immediate and eventual destinies of entire planets; in essence it could choose the Biological result of its home planet and other planets in its stellar system if they too have life.

Methods: A Type II Civilization evolves from a Type-I Civilization upon 90% or greater completion of a Dyson Sphere around its home star, yielding energy of e^26 Watts. It is ridiculously unlikely that such levels of energy would be achieved without a Dyson Sphere.

Ethics: This society would have access to a Bio-technical logic mechanism known as "The Monolith" Coined in the novel "2001: A Space Odyssey". This device would spread like a virus across countless star systems, mapping the galaxy and establishing information nodes for the Type II Civilization.

Methods: A Type III Civilization holds numerous Dyson Spheres across a single galaxy, having access to energy in the realm of e^37 Watts.

Ethics: This degree of reach would determine the destiny of its host galaxy and is at extreme risk for the evolution of one or many breakaway civilizations, which could technologically counter the original civilization. This problem is addressed in the novel "Foundation".

Methods: A Type IV Civilization would necessarily require access to time travel technology in order to capture and gather energy from the visible universe.

Ethics: Whether it be through wormholes, time bubbles, or organic antimatter, this civilization would cause unforseeable damage to the upper dimensions by its very existence. It would be unlikely for a civilization of this type to employ ethics in their inter-galactic conquest.

Methods: A Type V Civilization would require free movement in all temporal dimensions and would require energy from multiple realities. Such a society would have a vastly smaller population than all previous as the amount of energy required to fold through the precept dimensions is exponentially greater than the temporal dimensions.

Ethics: Creatures of this civilization can be referred to as "Kardashevian gods", or demi-gods, whose existence is inherently unethical. As such they are the natural enemy to all lower life. Ironically they could evolve from any organism.

Methods: Civilizations of Type VI and beyond are inherently contradictory to known physics. A "Kardashevian God" would exist outside the tenth dimension. Only the tenth dimension exists there, therefore, it would be the tenth dimension. It would comprise all things in all times across all sets of rules or "precept". Because of the impossibility of omnipotence, omnipresence, and omniscience such a being should be impossible.

Ethics: Unlike the Type V Civilization, a creature of this type cannot be measured ethically. This is because any action it takes is both for and against itself, a zero-sum game.
