TECHNOLOGICAL EVOLUTION

1. Invention and development improve methods and applications over time.

2. Nature contains complex mechanisms: natural and those produced through Biological Evolution.

a. These mechanisms can be analyzed for their methods and applications.

3. Invention and development will adopt and improve nature’s methods and applications over time.

Summary: Anything nature can do man can do better.

CREATORS AND CREATIONS

1. All known creators (humans) are currently mortal.

2. Creations (ideas and applications) can be transmitted through generations.

a. Creations can continue to influence future generations while a creator no longer can.

3. A creation can continue to be developed or evolve beyond its creator’s life time.

a. A creation could evolve to a state unrecognizable by its creator.

1. Creations that continue to evolve must do so because they have a great impact on civilization.

4. Some creations will have a greater impact on civilization than their creators.

5. Some creations are greater than their creators.

Summary: A creation may be greater than its creator.

ALTRUISM OF INTELLIGENT LIFE

Define altruism as a biological trait which increases the rate of selfless behaviors from an organism.

1. Altruism arises through Social Evolution which impacts Biological Evolution over a long time.

2. Social Evolution will increase or reduce altruism over time.

a. Biological Evolution could remove or instate altruism over a long time.

Define intelligence as a biological trait which allows abstract, non-instinctive conceptualization.

3. Intelligent creatures fluctuate in degree and presence of altruism over short and long periods of time, respectively.

a. Intelligence does not necessarily precede altruism, and altruism does not necessarily precede intelligence.

b. It is likely that intelligence increases the odds of evolving altruism and vice versa.

1. It is unknown if altruism will remain as a species becomes more intelligent than humans.

Assumption: Alien intelligences exist.

4. Alien intelligences may or may not be altruistic.

5. Humans are generally altruistic.

a. Humans are more likely to be altruistic than alien intelligences.

Summary: Humans are more likely to be trustworthy than alien intelligences.

TIME TRAVELERS

Define time travel as free movement in the 4th, 5th, and 6th dimensions.

Assumption: Time travel is possible.

1. Time travelers will one day exist.

a. Time travelers may freely move into the past.

2. Time travelers exist now.

Summary: If time travelers will ever exist then they exist now.

3. Human or human derived time travelers will one day exist.

4. Human or human derived time travelers exist now.

Summary: If human or human derived time travelers will ever exist then they exist now.

Refer to “Altruism of Intelligent Life”.

5. Human or human derived time travelers have an altruistic trait in their evolutionary past.

a. Human or human derived time travelers are more likely to be altruistic than alien time travelers.

Summary: Human or human derived time travelers are more likely to be trustworthy than other time travelers.

6. Human or human derived time travelers may or may not retain their altruism.

Summary: Modern humans are more likely to be trustworthy than human or human derived time travelers.

OMNIPOTENCE

Define omnipotence as the capability to complete any action or comprise any status.

1. Some statuses or capabilities are logical or definitional contradictions.

(e.g. mortality and immortality cannot be simultaneously achieved)

2. Some statuses or capabilities cannot be simultaneously achieved.

Summary: Omnipotence is impossible.

OMNISCIENCE

Define omniscience as the capability to know all things to the finest possible detail.

Simplification: The finest possible detail is at the atomic level.

Simplification: There is only one universe.

1. Imagine a computer (brain) derived of atoms, in which each atom was a binary bit.

2. The amount of feasible information at the atomic level would require at least one bit per atom.

(In actuality this number is far greater)

3. This computer must be greater than or equal to the size of the entire universe to contain all information in the universe.

4. This computer must be greater than or equal to the size of itself again to contain all information that its own structure represents.

5. This computer must be at least twice the size of the universe.

6. Such a computer is absurd as it is larger than all space.

Summary: Omniscience is impossible.

OMNIPRESENCE

Define omnipresence as occupation of all possible space in a three-dimensional universe.

1. An omnipresent being occupies the entire universe.

2. The universe occupies the entire universe.

3. It is absurd for two objects to occupy the same location.

Summary: Omnipresence is impossible.

2. The doctor might or may not understand the full effect of his treatments to the greatest possible precision.

a. Effects may lie outside his observational capacity.

1. Too much difference in scale or scope.

b. Effects may lie outside his mental capacity.

c. Effects may lie outside his theoretical concepts.

3. The doctor’s treatments will cause unexpected and expected results.

a. The unexpected results may remain latent.

1. Side symptoms

Refer to “TIME TRAVELERS”, “OMNIPOTENCE”, “OMNISCIENCE”, “OMNIPRESENCE”, AND “ALTRUISM OF INTELLIGENT LIFE”.

4. Imagine a time traveler who alters reality through intentional and unintentional means.

5. The time traveler cannot understand and might or may not be concerned with the full effect of his alterations to the greatest possible precision.

6. The time traveler’s alterations will cause unexpected and expected results.

a. The unexpected results may remain latent.

Consider the “Kardashev Scale”.

7. The time traveler requires energy on a far greater scale than pre-Kardashevian and Types I through IV civilizations.

a. The energy required to warp time is likely equivalent to the energy contained as time.

1. To consume time the time traveler must break time down to a less energetic state.

8. In obtaining this energy, the time traveler may damage the home universe of lower civilizations with or without knowledge and with or without concern.

Summary: If time travelers exist they might be affecting us now and may not be aware nor concerned.
